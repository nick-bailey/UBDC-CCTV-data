The easing of lockdown in Glasgow

Nick Bailey, UBDC - 28/04/2021

On Monday 26th April, some of the lockdown restrictions were eased in Scotland. In particular, non-essential shops were allowed to re-open, and pubs and restaurants could offer food inside. Similar changes had occurred in England two weeks earlier.

In Glasgow, we can look at the impacts of these changes thanks to a unique partnership between the Urban Big Data Centre, Glasgow City Council and the Glasgow Centre for Population Health. As explained in an earlier blog (https://www.ubdc.ac.uk/news-media/2020/july/creating-open-data-counts-of-pedestrians-and-vehicles-using-cctv-cameras/), UBDC is using spare capacity in the CCTV system to analyse images captured at regular intervals for set locations. Our software produces counts of the number of pedestrians present in these images. These data are then made publicly available providing a near-real-time picture of footall across the city.

Other high street locations

In addition to the city centre, we have data for high street locations such as Byres Road, Duke Street, Maryhill Road and Victoria Road.

Parks

One feature of the lockdown period has been the increased use of parks and opens spaces. With the opening up of alternatives such as shops and cafes, we might expect to see a drop in the usages of these spaces. That appears to be the case in Glasgow. The early days of easing have been marked by a drop in temperatures compared to the previous week but footfall is down on almost all the previous eight weeks. At last, people have other places they can be!

The riverfront

Glasgows riverfront has also been a popular destination over lockdown for walking, cycling or just hanging out.
